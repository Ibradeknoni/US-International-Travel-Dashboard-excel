# US International Travel Dashboard (Excel)

## Overview

This project analyzes international passenger travel from the United States using Microsoft Excel. The objective was to transform raw travel data into meaningful insights through data cleaning, exploratory data analysis (EDA), pivot table analysis, and dashboard visualization.

The dashboard provides a comprehensive overview of passenger movements across 46 international destinations over a 32-year period, helping uncover travel patterns, destination popularity, and seasonal travel trends.

## Business Problem

International travel generates large volumes of data that can be difficult to interpret without proper analysis and visualization. Understanding where passengers travel, when travel demand peaks, and which destinations account for the largest share of traffic can support decision-making within the travel and tourism industry.

This project explores historical travel data to answer key questions such as:

* Which destinations are most popular among U.S. travellers?
* How does travel demand vary throughout the year?
* Which periods experience the highest passenger traffic?
* How are passengers distributed across destinations?
* Which countries account for the largest share of international travel?

## Key Findings

### Top Travel Destinations

Canada recorded the highest passenger volume with approximately 601 million passengers, making it the most travelled international destination from the United States.

Other leading destinations included:

* Mexico
* United Kingdom
* Japan
* Germany
* France

These destinations consistently attracted a significant share of international travellers throughout the period analyzed.

### Monthly Travel Trends

Passenger activity varied throughout the year, with July and August recording the highest travel volumes. Both months exceeded 400 million passengers, indicating stronger international travel demand during peak summer periods.

### Quarterly Travel Trends

Passenger traffic remained relatively balanced across all four quarters. However, Quarter 2 and Quarter 3 each recorded more than 1 billion passengers, with Quarter 3 generating the highest passenger volume overall.

This pattern suggests that international travel demand is strongest during the middle of the year when holiday and vacation travel typically peak.

### Weekday Travel Patterns

Travel activity remained relatively consistent throughout the week. Sunday recorded the highest passenger volume, while Wednesday recorded the lowest, suggesting stronger travel demand around weekends.

### Destination Concentration

A relatively small number of destinations accounted for a substantial share of international passenger traffic.

* In 2020, Mexico, Canada, and the United Kingdom represented more than 43% of total passenger travel.
* In 2021, Mexico, the Dominican Republic, and Colombia accounted for more than 56% of total passenger travel.
